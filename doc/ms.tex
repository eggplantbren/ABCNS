\section{Bayesian inference}
By the product rule of probability theory, the prior $p(\params)$ and the
conditional prior for the data $p(\data | \params)$ imply a joint prior
for $\params$ and $\data$:
\begin{equation}
\begin{array}{lr}
p(\params, \data) = p(\params)p(\data|\params)
\end{array}
\end{equation}
